Two wild birds have tested positive for bird flu - the first cases in the wild in England, the UK's Chief Veterinary Officer has confirmed. The H5N8 strain of avian flu was found in Leicestershire and Somerset in two dead wigeons - a type of duck. It follows the discovery of the virus in the wild in Scotland and Wales and on a Lincolnshire farm last week. Chief vet Nigel Gibbens said the public risk was "very low" but experts would "strengthen surveillance" of the virus. "Today's confirmed findings mean that avian flu has now been found in wild birds in widely separated parts of England, Wales and Scotland," he added.
